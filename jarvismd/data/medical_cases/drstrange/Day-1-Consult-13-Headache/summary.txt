#### Presenting Complaint
  * Patient presents with a severe headache that has been ongoing for three days.

#### Observations
  * **History Of Presenting Complaint**  
    * Headache started around three days ago.
    * Pain is mainly around the right eye but feels like it's all over the head.
    * Headache is constant but varies in intensity, rated 8/10.
    * Pain is described as dull but can become sharp.
    * Headache has been worsening over the last three days.
    * Ibuprofen taken for the last two days with no relief.

  * **Past Medical History**  
    * No personal history of migraines.
    * Family history of migraines: mother and sister.

  * **Review Of Systems**  
    * Vision: No loss of vision, but experiences shooting lights and photophobia.
    * Gastrointestinal: Feels sick but no vomiting.
    * Musculoskeletal: No neck pain or stiffness.
    * Neurological: No difficulty with speech or weakness in arms and legs.
    * General: No fever or rashes.

  * **Current Medications**  
    * **Medication name**: Ibuprofen  
       **Formulation**:   
       **Dosage**: 3 tablets a day  
       **Usage frequency**:   

  * **Allergies**: Nil recorded.  
  * **Social History**  
    * Lives with three housemates.
    * Works in IT, finds it difficult to focus due to headache.
    * Wears glasses, no recent eye test.
    * Social smoker and drinks alcohol socially.
    * Recent stress at work due to a new project.

  * **Physical Examination**: Nil recorded.  

#### Assessment
  * The headache is likely a migraine, given the family history and the nature of the symptoms.

#### Plan
  * Increase ibuprofen dosage to 2 tablets, 3 times a day.
  * Consider prescribing naproxen as a stronger anti-inflammatory.
  * Add other painkillers like paracetamol or codeine if necessary.
  * Prescribe codeine as it has been effective for the patient's mother.
  * Recommend keeping a headache diary to identify potential triggers.
  * Follow-up in one to two weeks to discuss prophylactic medications if headaches persist.
  * Advise patient to return if symptoms worsen or if there are any new concerning symptoms like vision changes or weakness.
