## Recommendations for Further Diagnostic Steps

1. **Stool Culture and Sensitivity**
   - **Type**: Non-invasive
   - **Urgency**: If symptoms persist beyond 3-4 days
   - **Considerations**: Helps identify bacterial pathogens and guide antibiotic therapy if needed.
   - **Pros and Cons**: Pros: Identifies specific pathogens; Cons: May take several days for results.

2. **Electrolyte Panel**
   - **Type**: Non-invasive
   - **Urgency**: If signs of dehydration or persistent symptoms
   - **Considerations**: Assesses for electrolyte imbalances due to diarrhea and vomiting.
   - **Pros and Cons**: Pros: Quick results; Cons: Requires blood draw.

3. **Physical Examination**
   - **Type**: Non-invasive
   - **Urgency**: Immediate
   - **Considerations**: Assess for signs of dehydration, abdominal tenderness, and other potential complications.
   - **Pros and Cons**: Pros: Immediate assessment; Cons: May require follow-up tests based on findings.
