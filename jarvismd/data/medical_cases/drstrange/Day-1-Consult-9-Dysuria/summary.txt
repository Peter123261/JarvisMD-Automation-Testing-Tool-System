#### Presenting Complaint
  * Pain and discomfort while urinating, ongoing for a few days.

#### Observations
  * **History Of Presenting Complaint**  
    * Pain started a few days ago.
    * Described as a stinging pain during urination.
    * Occasional lower abdominal pain, described as cramping but different from menstrual cramps.
    * Noticed a small amount of blood in urine earlier today.
    * Urine has an unusual smell.

  * **Review Of Systems**  
    * General: No fever, no nausea, no vomiting.
    * Gastrointestinal: No diarrhoea, no loose stools.
    * Genitourinary: Pain during urination, occasional lower abdominal pain, no increased frequency of urination, no other discharge.

       **Formulation**:

    * Social alcohol consumption on weekends.
    * Non-smoker.
    * Sexually active with a regular partner, uses condoms for contraception.

  * **Physical Examination**: nil recorded  

#### Assessment
  * Likely diagnosis of a simple urinary tract infection (UTI).

#### Plan
  * Prescribe a 3-day course of antibiotics (nitrofurantoin or trimethoprim), 1 tablet twice a day.
  * Advise to drink plenty of fluids to help flush out the infection.
  * Suggest cranberry juice to help with symptoms.
  * If symptoms persist or worsen by the end of the week, patient should call back or return for further evaluation.
  * Monitor for high temperatures, back pain, or feeling generally unwell, which could indicate the infection spreading to the kidneys.
  * Advise to avoid sexual activity until symptoms clear up, continue using barrier contraception.
