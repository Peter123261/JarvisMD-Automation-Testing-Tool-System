## Recommendations for Further Diagnostic Steps

1. **Stool Culture and Sensitivity**
   - **Test Type**: Non-invasive
   - **Urgency**: High
   - **Patient-specific considerations**: Identifies causative pathogen, guides antibiotic therapy.
   - **Pros and Cons**: Pros: Identifies specific pathogen; Cons: Takes time for results.

2. **Oral Rehydration Therapy**
   - **Test Type**: Non-invasive
   - **Urgency**: Immediate
   - **Patient-specific considerations**: Prevents dehydration, easy to administer.
   - **Pros and Cons**: Pros: Effective for rehydration; Cons: May not be sufficient if severe dehydration is present.

3. **Empiric Antibiotic Therapy**
   - **Test Type**: Medication
   - **Urgency**: Consider if bacterial infection is suspected
   - **Patient-specific considerations**: Use if symptoms are severe or if stool culture indicates bacterial infection.
   - **Pros and Cons**: Pros: Can quickly address bacterial infection; Cons: Risk of antibiotic resistance, side effects.
