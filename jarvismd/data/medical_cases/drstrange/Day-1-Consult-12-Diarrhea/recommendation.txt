## Recommendations for Further Diagnostic Steps

1. **Stool Culture**
   - **Type**: Non-invasive
   - **Urgency**: Within the next 24-48 hours if symptoms persist
   - **Considerations**: Rule out bacterial causes of gastroenteritis.
   - **Pros**: Identifies specific pathogens, guides targeted treatment.
   - **Cons**: May take a few days for results.

2. **Electrolyte Panel**
   - **Type**: Non-invasive (blood test)
   - **Urgency**: As soon as possible if signs of dehydration are present
   - **Considerations**: Assess for electrolyte imbalances due to diarrhea.
   - **Pros**: Provides information on hydration status and need for IV fluids.
   - **Cons**: Requires blood draw.

3. **IV Fluids**
   - **Type**: Invasive
   - **Urgency**: Immediate if signs of severe dehydration are present
   - **Considerations**: Rehydrate and stabilize the patient.
   - **Pros**: Rapid rehydration and symptom relief.
   - **Cons**: Requires clinical setting for administration.
